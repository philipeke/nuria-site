2. What Data We Collect and Why

2.1 Account Data

When you create an account we collect your email address and, optionally, a display name. This is required to provide you with the App, associate your credit balance and settings with your account, deliver subscription entitlements, and communicate with you regarding your account. Without a valid email address you cannot create a Nuria account.

2.2 Purchase and Subscription Data

Optional purchases and subscriptions — including the Barakah support subscription and any other paid digital features offered inside the App — are processed exclusively by Apple Inc. (App Store) or Google LLC (Google Play) using their own payment infrastructure. OakDev & AI AB does not receive, store, or process your payment card details, bank account information, or any other financial instrument data at any point.

We receive from Apple or Google a purchase receipt token, a transaction or order identifier, the product identifier purchased, and the purchase or renewal timestamp. We use this metadata solely to:

  • Verify that a purchase is valid and has not been used fraudulently.
  • Apply starter credits, subscriber credits, or other entitlements to your account.
  • Determine whether your Barakah subscription is currently active and enable or disable the relevant features accordingly.
  • Detect and investigate suspected fraudulent or duplicate purchase claims.
  • Maintain minimum accounting records as required by Swedish law.

We store only the minimum purchase metadata needed for those purposes. Apple and Google act as independent controllers of your payment data; their respective privacy policies govern that processing: Apple Privacy Policy · Google Privacy Policy. In-app purchases are also subject to each platform's terms, including Apple's standard Licensed Application End User License Agreement (EULA) where applicable, and the Google Play Terms of Service on Android.

2.3 Push Notification Token

If you grant permission for push notifications — for example to receive alerts for prayer times (Adhan reminders) — your device's push notification token (also called a device token or registration ID) is collected and stored on our servers. This token is issued by Apple Push Notification service (APNs) or Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) and is specific to your device and app installation.

  • Purpose: The token is used solely to route push notifications (such as prayer-time reminders) to your specific device.
  • No location transmitted: Prayer-time calculation for Adhan notifications is performed on-device. We do not receive your location in order to send notifications; we may store a time-zone or city-level region preference if you have explicitly configured one within the App, and only to schedule notification delivery at the correct local time.
  • Revocation: You may revoke push notification permission at any time in your device's operating system settings. Upon revocation, your token will no longer be used to deliver notifications. You may also disable specific prayer-time notifications within the App settings without revoking the general permission.
  • Token refresh: Notification tokens can be refreshed by the operating system at any time. When a new token is issued, the old token is replaced in our systems.

2.4 Reflection History

The personalised reflections you request and receive through the App — whether a category-based reflection or an Ask Nuria exchange — are stored server-side and linked to your account so you can review them within the App and so we can manage your credit balance accurately. This content may reflect your chosen category (for example Gratitude, Strength, Evening Reflection, or Iman & Trust) but does not include any additional personal profiling beyond what you explicitly request.

2.5 Usage Data

We collect anonymised and pseudonymised data about how you interact with the App — such as which features are accessed, session frequency, which reflection categories are selected, and which sections of the App are opened. This data is aggregated and is used exclusively to understand usage patterns and improve the product. It is not used to build individual user profiles, is not linked to personally identifiable information, and is never used for advertising.

2.6 Device and Technical Data

We collect your device type (e.g. iPhone, Android phone), operating system version, app version, and a resettable app-instance identifier. This information is necessary for app functionality, security, feature compatibility testing, and resolving technical issues reported by users.

2.7 Crash and Diagnostic Reports

If the App crashes or encounters a critical error, anonymised diagnostic information (including stack traces and the device state at the moment of the crash) is collected to help us identify and fix bugs. We use Firebase Crashlytics (Google LLC) for crash reporting. No account-level personal identifiers are included in crash reports beyond what is technically unavoidable (for example a pseudonymous installation ID). See Section 6 for more on Crashlytics.

2.8 Support Communications

When you contact us by email or through any form on this website, we retain your name, email address, and the content of your message in order to respond to you and maintain a record of our communications. We may retain notes relating to your support case for as long as is reasonably necessary to resolve the matter and handle any related follow-up.

2.9 Location Data (Qibla & Prayer Times)

When you use the Qibla Compass or Prayer Times features, the App requests access to your device's location via the standard permission granted by your operating system. This is governed by the following principles:

  • What is accessed: Your device's approximate or precise GPS coordinates, as authorised by you through the iOS or Android permission dialogue.
  • Purpose: Location data is used solely to calculate the direction toward the Kaaba in Mecca (Qibla) and to determine accurate prayer times (Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha) based on standard astronomical and geographic formulae for your current position.
  • On-device processing: All Qibla and prayer-time calculations are performed entirely on your device. Your GPS coordinates are never transmitted to our servers, are not stored in your account, and are not shared with any third party for any purpose whatsoever.
  • Permission prompt: The App will request your location permission before these features activate. You may grant "While Using the App" or "Always" permission (iOS) or the equivalent on Android, depending on whether you wish notifications for prayer times at your current location.
  • Revocation: You may revoke location permission at any time via your device's operating system settings. Revoking permission disables the Qibla Compass and Prayer Times features but does not affect any other App functionality.
  • Legal basis: Consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR), granted through your device-level permission. You may withdraw consent at any time by revoking the location permission in device settings.

2.10 Ask Nuria — Free-Form Questions

When you use the Ask Nuria feature (available to active Barakah subscribers and other eligible users as shown in the App), you type a free-form question and submit it to receive a personalised AI-generated response rooted in the Sunni Islamic tradition. Regarding this data:

  • What is collected: The exact text of the question you submit and the AI-generated answer returned to you are both stored on our servers and linked to your account.
  • How the AI is powered: Your question text is transmitted to our AI service provider (see Section 6) to generate a response. The AI provider processes your query as a data processor acting under our instructions and subject to a binding data processing agreement.
  • Sensitive content: Questions you submit may touch on personal matters, spiritual struggles, family circumstances, or religious beliefs. This content may constitute special category personal data under Article 9 GDPR insofar as it reveals your religious beliefs or personal life. See Section 3 for how we handle this data.
  • Quality improvement: We may review a de-identified sample of Ask Nuria questions and answers to monitor response quality and to refine prompting and content filters. No individual user is identified in this process.
  • What we do not do: We do not use the content of your questions to build advertising profiles, do not sell them, and do not share them with third parties for any commercial purpose outside of what is described in this policy.

2.11 Partner Programme, Nuria Partner Accounts, and Operational Partner Profiles

Nuria operates a Partner Programme through which Islamic organisations, mosques, community groups, and other partners may receive a unique referral code to share with their communities. When a new user installs the App via a partner referral link or enters a referral code:

  • What is collected (referral attribution): The referral code used, the date of first activation, and whether the referred user completes account registration. We do not collect additional personal details of the referred user beyond what is already collected under Section 2.1.
  • Partner-level contact and statistics: If you are a registered partner (community or organisation), we collect the contact details you provided when joining the programme (name, organisation name, email address) and aggregate statistics about activations attributed to your referral code (number of activations, dates). We do not share the personal data of individual referred users with partners.
  • Purpose (referrals): The referral data is used to attribute registrations to the correct partner, to manage the partner relationship, to send programme-related communications to partners, and to report aggregate (non-personal) programme statistics to partners.
  • Legal basis (referrals): Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) for partners who have signed up to the programme; legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) for tracking referral attribution in aggregate.
  • Retention (general partner contact): Partner contact details are retained for the duration of the partner relationship and for 3 years thereafter, except where a longer period is required for accounting, tax, or anti-fraud purposes as described below.

Nuria Partner user accounts and Firebase UID linkage

A Nuria Partner in the operational sense is a person or organisation representative who maintains a Nuria user account (the same account system as end users of the App) registered with a specific email address. For programme administration, we may record which referral code is associated with which partner email address and, where technically available, the corresponding Firebase Authentication user ID (UID) for that email address.

We do not assign arbitrary UIDs to partners. The UID we store is the identifier returned by our authentication backend when we verify that the partner email address corresponds to an existing Nuria account. If the email cannot be matched to an account, the referral code may remain linked to the email address in an unverified state until the partner registers or the mapping is corrected. Partners are responsible for ensuring that the email address they provide is accurate, belongs to them or their authorised representative, and matches the Nuria account they intend to use.

Operational partner profile (payout, tax, and banking details)

Where we offer or agree to provide revenue share, fees, reimbursements, or other payments to a partner under a separate commercial arrangement (or where we otherwise need legal and payment details to operate the programme), we may collect and store additional financial and identity-related information about the partner. This may include, without limitation: contact telephone number; postal address; country; company registration or VAT identification number where applicable; and bank account details such as account holder name, bank name, domestic account number, and IBAN. This data is processed solely for legitimate programme administration, including:

  • Verifying the identity of the payee and the correctness of payment instructions;
  • Executing payments, reconciling ledgers, and preparing payout reports;
  • Complying with applicable accounting, tax, anti–money laundering, sanctions, and regulatory reporting obligations.

Such operational partner profile data is stored on our secure backend infrastructure (including Google Cloud Firestore as part of our Firebase environment) in restricted administrative configuration records, keyed by referral code and cross-referenced to the partner email and verified UID where available. It is not displayed on public marketing pages and is not accessible to other Nuria users. Access is limited to authorised personnel and automated systems under strict access controls and audit logging.

Special categories: This operational profile does not typically include sensitive categories under Article 9 GDPR in isolation; however, if you voluntarily include information in free-text fields that reveals religious belief, health, or other special categories, we will treat that content in line with Section 3 and only process it where permitted by law.

Legal basis (operational profile): Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) where we have a contractual or partner relationship requiring payment; compliance with legal obligations (Art. 6(1)(c)) for accounting and tax records; and legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) in preventing fraud, abuse, and duplicate payments in the Partner Programme.

Retention (financial and operational records): Banking, payout, and reconciliation records may be retained for up to 7 years from the end of the financial year to which they relate, in line with Swedish accounting and tax requirements, or longer where required by law or to resolve disputes. Aggregate referral statistics are retained indefinitely where they contain no personal data.

2.12 What We Do Not Collect

We do not collect contacts from your address book, camera data, microphone data, or any biometric data. We do not transmit or store your GPS location on our servers (Section 2.9).   • Reflection history: The content of personalised reflections you have received is stored in your account history.
  • Ask Nuria question and answer history: Free-form questions you submit through Ask Nuria may contain personal spiritual matters, religious questions, or information about your beliefs, emotional circumstances, or life situations.
  • Fact of App use: The mere fact that you use a Sunni Islamic app may itself indicate your religious affiliation or beliefs.

We process this special category data exclusively on the basis of your explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a) GDPR), which you provide by creating a Nuria account and actively using these features. You may withdraw this consent at any time by deleting your account (which removes all personal data subject to the retention obligations described in Section 8).

We do not share your reflection history, category usage, or submitted questions with any third party for any purpose other than:

  • Operating the App and its features (e.g. transmitting your Ask Nuria question to our AI provider to generate a response, subject to a binding data processing agreement), and
  • Complying with legal obligations where required.

We will never sell, license, or use your special category data for advertising, research beyond App improvement, or any purpose that has not been disclosed in this policy.

5. How We Use Your Data

Specifically, we use your personal data to:

  • Create, authenticate, and maintain your Nuria account and ensure it remains secure.
  • Verify purchases made through Apple App Store or Google Play and apply any credits, subscription entitlements (including Barakah benefits), or other paid digital features linked to your account.
  • Generate and deliver personalised spiritual reflections based on your chosen category, drawing one credit per request from your balance.
  • Process free-form questions submitted via Ask Nuria (by transmitting them to our AI provider), generate AI-powered responses rooted in the Sunni Islamic tradition, and return those responses to you.
  • Store your complete reflection history — including Ask Nuria question and answer pairs — so you can review them within the App at any time.
  • Calculate and display your local prayer times (Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha) using your device's GPS coordinates, processed entirely on-device and never transmitted to our servers.
  • Display the Qibla direction toward the Kaaba in Mecca using your device's compass and GPS coordinates, processed entirely on-device.
  • Deliver daily content (daily Quranic verse, role model of the day, daily reflection, Islamic calendar events, and similar curated content) to your App home screen.
  • Send push notifications, including prayer-time Adhan reminders (if you have enabled this) and transactional notifications (for example, when a purchase is confirmed).
  • Manage the Partner Programme, including attributing new registrations to the correct partner referral code; communicating aggregate programme statistics to registered partners; verifying partner email addresses against Nuria user accounts; storing Firebase UIDs for matched accounts where available; and processing operational partner profile data (including payout and banking details) solely for programme administration, reconciliation, and legal compliance.
  • Respond to your support enquiries and process account deletion requests.
  • Improve the App through analysis of anonymised, aggregated usage data.
  • Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents.
  • Comply with applicable legal and regulatory obligations, including tax and accounting requirements under Swedish law.

We will never use your data for targeted advertising, behavioural profiling for commercial purposes, or any purpose incompatible with the above list.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for no longer than necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, subject to mandatory legal retention obligations. Specific retention periods are:

  • Account and profile data (email, display name): Retained for the lifetime of your account. Upon account deletion, removed from active systems within 30 days.
  • Reflection history (including Ask Nuria exchanges): Retained for the lifetime of your account. Removed within 30 days of account deletion.
  • Credit balance and transaction log: Retained for the lifetime of your account. Removed within 30 days of account deletion, subject to the accounting retention rule below.
  • Purchase receipt metadata (transaction IDs, product IDs, dates, amounts): Retained for 7 years from the date of each transaction, in compliance with Swedish accounting law (Bokföringslagen 1999:1078), even after account deletion. Only the minimum data required for accounting records is retained; your email address is retained in association with these records for the same 7-year period for the purpose of linking them to the relevant accounting entries if required.
  • Push notification tokens: Retained while your account is active and notifications are enabled. Deleted within 30 days of account deletion, or immediately upon revocation of notification permission if you explicitly disable all notifications.
  • Partner programme contact data (general): Retained for the duration of the partner relationship and for 3 years thereafter to handle disputes or follow-up relating to the programme, unless a longer period applies under the rule below.
  • Partner payout, banking, tax, and reconciliation records: May be retained for up to 7 years from the end of the financial year to which they relate (or longer where required by law), in line with Section 2.11.
  • Support communications: Retained for up to 3 years after the last communication in the thread, unless you request earlier deletion and no overriding legal basis prevents us from complying.
  • Crash reports: Retained for up to 12 months from the date of the crash event.
  • Anonymised aggregate usage data: May be retained indefinitely, as it contains no personal identifiers and cannot be used to identify any individual.

To request early deletion of your account and data, use the in-App deletion flow (Settings → Account → Delete Account) or submit a request at our deletion page or by emailing hello@oakdev.app.

9. Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject, you have the following rights under the GDPR. To exercise any of them, contact us at hello@oakdev.app with your request and, where necessary, sufficient information to identify your account. We will respond within one calendar month (extendable by a further two months for complex requests, with notice) at no charge to you.

  • Right of access (Art. 15): You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, along with information about how it is processed, the categories of data held, recipients of the data, and the retention periods applied.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16): You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you. You may update your display name directly in the App settings at any time.
  • Right to erasure / "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17): You may request that we delete your personal data, subject to any overriding legal retention obligations (for example, the 7-year accounting retention rule for purchase records). Account deletion can be initiated in-App or via our deletion page.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18): In certain circumstances (for example, while you contest the accuracy of data or have objected to processing and a determination is pending), you may request that we restrict active processing of your data to storage only.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20): Where processing is based on contract or consent and is carried out by automated means, you may request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (for example, JSON or CSV). You may also ask us to transmit it directly to another controller where technically feasible.
  • Right to object (Art. 21): You may object to processing carried out on the basis of our legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)). We will cease that processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights, and freedoms, or that the processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)): Where we rely on consent as the legal basis — including explicit consent for special category data — you may withdraw that consent at any time by deleting your account, revoking device-level permissions (location, notifications), or contacting us. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of any processing carried out before the withdrawal.
  • Right not to be subject to solely automated decision-making (Art. 22): We do not make solely automated decisions about you that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

If you are not satisfied with our response to any rights request, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Swedish supervisory authority (see Section 14) or any EU supervisory authority competent for your place of habitual residence or work.

10. Security

  • Encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher for all network communications between the App and our backend.
  • Encryption of sensitive data at rest using industry-standard algorithms on our cloud infrastructure.
  • Role-based access controls ensuring that only authorised personnel can access personal data, and only to the extent necessary for their role.
  • Firebase Security Rules restricting read and write access to your account data so that only your authenticated session can access your personal records.
  • Periodic review of our security posture and third-party processor agreements.
  • Incident response procedures for personal data breaches, including assessment of risk and mandatory notification to the supervisory authority within 72 hours and to affected individuals without undue delay, as required by GDPR Articles 33–34.

While we take data security seriously and strive to protect your data, no digital system is completely immune from risk. In the event of a personal data breach that poses a high risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you without undue delay and provide clear information about the nature of the breach and the steps we are taking.

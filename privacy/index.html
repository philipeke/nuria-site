2.3 Anti-abuse device binding (starter reflections and legacy trial checks)

To prevent a single physical device from claiming repeated starter reflection grants or abusing legacy free-trial mechanics across multiple accounts, the App uses device-binding records and, for legacy trial flows, a salted irreversible device fingerprint hash.

  • Starter reflections: The one-time 15 reflection starter grant for free users is tracked per device binding in device_starters/{bindingId} so reinstalling the App or creating additional accounts on the same device cannot obtain multiple starter grants.
  • Legacy trial fingerprint (where still applicable): For legacy subscription trial enrolment, the client computes SHA-256(salt || platformId) where platformId is derived from on-device identifiers combined with the device model. The raw platformId never leaves your device; only the hash is stored at device_fingerprints/{hash}.
  • Purpose: Prevent fraud and abuse of free starter credits and legacy trial offers. These records are used solely for anti-abuse evaluation and investigation.
  • Irreversibility: Fingerprint hashes cannot be reversed to identify the underlying device and do not on their own personally identify you.
  • Persistence by design: Anti-abuse records intentionally survive account deletion so the signal remains effective. Fingerprint records are automatically purged 365 days after the most recent relevant attempt.
  • Right to erasure: You may request earlier deletion of a fingerprint or starter-binding record by writing to hello@nuria.one. We will assess and honour valid erasure requests in accordance with Sections 9 and 8.
  • Legal basis: Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) in preventing fraud and abuse, balanced against the minimal privacy impact of one-way hashes and device-binding records with limited retention.

When you use the Qibla Compass or Prayer Times features, the App requests access to your device's location via the standard permission granted by your operating system. This is governed by the following principles:

  • Permission prompt: The App will request your location permission before these features activate. You may grant "While Using the App" or "Always" permission (iOS) or the equivalent on Android, depending on whether you wish notifications for prayer times at your current location.
  • Revocation: You may revoke location permission at any time via your device's operating system settings. Revoking permission disables the Qibla Compass and Prayer Times features but does not affect any other App functionality.
  • Legal basis: Consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR), granted through your device-level permission. You may withdraw consent at any time by revoking the location permission in device settings.

10. Security

20. Account Deletion

You may delete your Nuria account at any time. There are three ways to do so:

  • In-App: Settings → Account → Delete Account.
  • Website: nuria.one/delete-account.
  • Email: hello@nuria.one from the address registered to your account.

20.1 What is deleted on account deletion

  • Account record (email, display name, Firebase Authentication user).
  • Reflection history, including Ask Nuria question/answer pairs.
  • Subscription entitlement state (iap_entitlements/{uid}).
  • Push notification tokens.
  • Family Deen Tree membership, Halqa membership, legacy bequest drafts and sealed items (and associated voice files in Cloud Storage).
  • Feature preferences (scholar path, theme, achievement progress, Qaida progress, Sunnat progress).
  • Support thread linkage to your identity.

Deletion completes within 30 days. Subscription cancellation must additionally be performed in the App Store or Google Play. Deleting your Nuria account does not cancel an Apple or Google subscription, only the platform store can do so.

20.2 What is retained after account deletion (and why)

  • Purchase receipt metadata (iap_transactions): retained for 7 years from each transaction under Swedish accounting law (Bokföringslagen 1999:1078).
  • Device fingerprint / starter-binding records: retained for anti-abuse purposes (typically up to 365 days after the latest relevant attempt). Hashes cannot be reversed to identify your device. Earlier deletion is available on request.
  • Anonymised aggregate analytics: retained indefinitely; not personally identifiable.
  • Records reasonably necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims (Art. 6(1)(f), Art. 9(2)(f) GDPR): retained only for as long as such purposes require.
  • Backup snapshots: standard Google Cloud backup snapshots may briefly contain residual copies of deleted records; these are purged according to Google's backup rotation policy.

Device-local Nuria Legacy content (Vault, Akhirah wishes, timed messages, Muhasaba, Deen Journey) is not removed by us when you delete your remote account, because it is stored on your device. To remove it, uninstall the App or clear app data through your operating system.

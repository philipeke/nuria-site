How Nuria is built

Sources & Standards

Every verse, dua, prayer tool, and guidance flow in Nuria should rest on a clear foundation. This page explains where the app's core content comes from and how we think about care, privacy, and accuracy.

Verified texts
Privacy-first design
Human editorial review

A trustworthy foundation

Built on trusted Islamic references, careful product work, and privacy-conscious engineering.

Nuria combines established Islamic texts, editorial review, and on-device calculations where privacy matters. We aim to be transparent about what comes from classical sources, what reflects product guidance, and what depends on device sensors or AI.

Authenticity first

We try to keep sacred texts, supplications, and religious references anchored in established Sunni material rather than vague summaries or trend-driven rewrites.

Privacy by default

When a feature can be calculated on your device, we prefer that approach. This is especially important for prayer times, Qibla, and personal spiritual use.

Clarity over overclaiming

If something depends on AI, location, or device sensors, we say so clearly and encourage verification with trusted local scholars or mosques when precision matters.

Q

Quran text and translations

The Arabic Quran text is based on verified Quran datasets such as Tanzil, together with established translation datasets used in trusted Islamic archives. We handle presentation changes carefully and avoid casual edits to the text itself.

D

Duas and adhkar

Supplications and remembrance collections are grounded in recognised works such as Hisnul Muslim and classical hadith collections including Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, and Sunan Abu Dawud.

P

Prayer times and Qibla

Prayer times and Qibla direction are calculated on your device using standard astronomical and geographic formulas, your location, and compass data. For mosque schedules or critical observance, always verify locally.

AI

Ask Nuria and AI guidance

Ask Nuria is designed to respond with guidance rooted in the Quran, Hadith, and mainstream Sunni teaching. It is meant to support reflection and comfort, not to invent rulings or replace qualified scholars.

A

Recitations, audio, and talks

Quran recitations and selected audio are sourced from licensed, public, or otherwise rights-respecting providers, including sources such as EveryAyah where appropriate.

R

Reflections and editorial content

Daily reflections and learning content are written and reviewed with the Quran, Hadith, tafsir, Riyad as-Salihin, and other classical references as their foundation.

A careful boundary

Prayer times, Qibla direction, and AI answers are built to support daily practice, but they do not replace your local mosque, qualified scholars, or formal religious rulings.

See something that looks wrong?

If you notice a typo, source issue, broken reference, or anything that feels off, contact us. We review reports carefully and correct verified issues as quickly as we can.
